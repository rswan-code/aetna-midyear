Stage 1
Member Response
Confirming strong reach into priority populations
Targeted outreach is effectively converting priority populations into program enrollment. 4,701 members entered this program between November and June — not contacts, not outreach attempts, but members who spoke with a Pyx care team member, shared what was happening in their lives, and made the decision to let us help. In Oklahoma, 3 in 4 members contacted enrolled — and two-thirds of them carry the specific diagnosis the plan targeted. In Michigan, nearly 1 in 2 maternal members reached came into the program. In Texas, diabetic members responded at more than twice the typical Medicaid engagement rate. These members weren't disengaged. They hadn't been reached the right way before.

Stage 2
Member Engagement
Engagement is deeper, more frequent, and care-team led
More enrolled members are engaging directly with the care team, and that engagement is deeper than before. In Oklahoma — the market with the most longitudinal data — 91% of enrolled members are now talking directly to a care team member, compared to 62% under the prior model. Calls average 31 minutes, nearly double the prior length. Each member receives 3 touchpoints on average — enough to build a care plan, identify barriers, and begin navigating toward care. This is not a campaign result. It's a model-level change: onboarding now centers on identifying member needs upfront, which informs a care plan and guides ongoing engagement. As a result, engagement is intentional and care-team led — not app-based or reactive.

Talking Points — Member Engagement

  • The shift from 62% to 91% direct engagement is not incremental improvement — it's a structural change in how members are served. The prior model waited for members to engage. This model goes to them, with a plan.
  • 3 calls per member averaging 31 minutes each is more than 90 minutes of direct care conversation per enrolled member. That's enough time to know someone's barriers, build a care plan, and start navigating them toward action.
  • More than half of enrolled members have now sustained multi-month engagement. The members staying in active care relationships are the ones most likely to activate into care next.
3
Stage 3
Deep Engagement
Sustained engagement uncovers barriers that traditional outreach misses
Through repeated interactions, members surface challenges — transportation gaps, medication barriers, childcare constraints, safety concerns — that don't appear in claims data and aren't identified through traditional outreach. You can't solve barriers you can't see. Now we can see them, at scale. In Oklahoma, the average barrier identification rate increased by 5.75 points versus the prior model — not because the barriers are new, but because deeper engagement creates the trust and time needed for members to share them. Transportation identified in 25% of members. Medication adherence in 38%. These are the barriers that explain why members weren't keeping appointments — and now the care team knows, and can act.
Oklahoma — Barrier Identification
New model vs. prior contract · +5.75 average point increase in barrier identification

Talking Points — Deep Engagement

  • Transportation identified in 25% of Oklahoma members — up from 13%. These aren't new barriers. They existed before. The prior model just wasn't surfacing them. Now the care team knows, and can act.
  • Medication adherence at 38% — up from 32%. For a BH and high-utilizer population, non-adherence is the single most preventable driver of avoidable hospitalization. Identifying it is the prerequisite to resolving it.
  • Standard outreach identifies barriers at the point of first contact — one interaction, one data point. Pyx identifies barriers through sustained engagement over time. More depth, more truth, more opportunity to intervene.
  • This is a clinical intelligence layer the plan didn't have before. It's not engagement reporting — it's a population needs map that informs care coordination priorities in every market.
4
Stage 4
Activation
Members are taking action to access care
As a result of sustained engagement and barrier resolution, members are now accessing care. Members who weren't completing recommended visits are now completing them. Texas leads the portfolio at 88% — nearly 9 in 10 enrolled diabetic members have completed a care visit. Michigan's maternal members are completing postpartum visits at 86%, compared to a national Medicaid average of approximately 62%. These are early behavior changes, and they are the leading indicators of improved plan quality performance. The completed visits happening today are the quality measures that close tomorrow.
What activation means for each program type
BH (FL, OK, IL): Completed a behavioral health visit, therapy session, or PCP follow-up — the visits that reduce crisis utilization and close BH care gaps.
Diabetic (TX): Completed a diabetes-related care visit — A1C monitoring, blood pressure, retinal exam, PCP management. Directly connected to HEDIS chronic condition measures.
Maternal (MI, LA): Completed a prenatal or postpartum visit — closing HEDIS postpartum gap measures and reducing maternal morbidity risk.
Cessation (KY): Set a quit date or completed a cessation milestone — documented behavior change, not just engagement.
All markets at a glance

Talking Points — Activation

  • Texas at 88% is the program benchmark — it shows what this model delivers when the population is clearly defined and engagement has depth. Every other market is moving toward that number.
  • Michigan's 86% maternal visit completion significantly outperforms the national Medicaid postpartum average of ~62%. In a state where maternal health quality is a plan priority, this is a reportable outcome.
  • 32 quit dates in Kentucky is a clinical commitment — not a goal set, not an intent captured. Each one represents a member on a path toward cessation who wasn't on that path before engagement.
  • Oklahoma's 128 completed appointments — across specialist, PCP, therapy, and maternal visits — represent 128 instances where a high-cost member chose a scheduled visit instead of an unplanned ED visit.
